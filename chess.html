how to get good at chess

the first moves

Every chess player is different, but all of the experienced chess players know what to do for the first few moves.
We all generally start out with getting our pawns into the center of the board,
as this gives us more control in the center of the board quickly
and gets our queen and biship ready to move.
We can also move our knights into the middle position as this will give us more control too.

power positions

The best defense is a good offense so let's get to know how to attack.
The best way to attack is to put your pieces in positions of general power throughout the board.
These positions are shown in the image, some of them require the queen to be moved or the piece to be covered by another piece.
Keep in mind, these positions are only good if your opponent remains relatively dormant and doesn't move to interfere with these,
but the best power positions are very game specific and are often very unique.
The best way to see these is with overall practice.

the start of a game

The most common mistakes that I see in chess are in the beginning and middle portions of the game.
The image above shows the optimum positioning for the knights, biships and center pawns at the beginning of the game.
Notice how the pieces are all in the middle and are designed to control the middle of the board/
The reason for this is that the middle of the board is the most important part of the board because it allows for your units to get into offensive positions.
You want your units in an offensive position, so that they can attack the opponent and at the same time keep their units at bay.
If you are sucessful in keeping your pieces in an offensive position you can dominate the field and take a lot of your opponent's pieces, allowing you to win a lot easier.
Also, castling is a very exellent defensive play when you are in a pickle.

practice makes perfect

The best thing to do to improve your chess game is simply to practice.
Practice, along with making mistakes can improve your chess eye and allow you to see what your opponent can do in their next turn.
Losing can teach you how to improve your strategy by showing you what sorts of moves that your opponent's stategy had aginst you.
Later, when you face that strategy again you know what kinds of moves to do or not do in preperation for those moves that you lost to earlier.
However, I can't just give practice to you through an online website, so go out there and play chess.

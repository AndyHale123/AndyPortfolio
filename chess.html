how to get good at chess

the first moves

Every chess player is different, but all of the exerienced chess players know what to do for the first few moves.
We all generally start out with getting our pawns into the center of the board,
as this gives us more control in the center of the board quickly
and gets our queen and bishup ready to move.
We can also move our kinghts into the middle position as this will give us more control too.

power positions

the best defence is a good offense so lets get to know how to attack.
the best way to attack is to put your pieces in positions of general power thought the board.
these positions are shown in the image, some of them reuire the queen to be moved or the piece to be covered by another piece.
keep in mind, these positions are only good if you opponent remains relitively dormant and dosen't move to interfer with these,
but the best power positions are very game specific and are often very unique
the best way to see these is with overall practice.

the start of a game

the most common mistakes that I see in chess are in the begining and middle protions of the game
the image above shows the opimum positioning for the knights, bishups and center pawns at the beginning of the game
notice how the pieces are all in the middle and are designed to control the middle of the board
the reason for this is that the middle of the board is the most important part of the board because it allows for your units to get into offensive positions
you want your units in an offensive position so that they can attack the opponet and at the same time keep their units at bay
if you are sucessful in keeping your pieces in an offensive position you can dominate the field and take a lot of your opponet's pieces, allowing you to win a lot easier.
also, castling is a very exelent defensive play when you are in a pickle

practice makes perfect

The best thing to do to improve your chess game is simply to practice
practice, along with making mistakes, can improve your chess eye and allow you to see what your opponet can do in their next turn.
Loseing can teach you how to improve your stratigy by showing you what sorts of moves that your oppoenst statigy had aginst you.
Later, when you face that stratigy again you know what kinds of moves to do or not do in preperation for those move that you lost to earlier.
